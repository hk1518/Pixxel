Competitions

(in no particular order)

1. Planet Labs

  1. Founded in 2010. Based in the USA.
  1. ARR ~ 52M
  1. ~200 satellites (Dove) in orbit. Hyperspectral imaging satellites & also analytics platform.
  1. Mission - To image the Whole world every day, making changes visible, accessible, and actionable.
  1. Spatial resolution & GSD~3.7m/px
  1. 2 kinds of Satellites - Dove / PlanetScope (180+) giving daily coverage of Earth. And 21 SkySat- high-resolution imaging satellites. Customers can go in and task this to go to a specific location on demand and request images (spatial resolution ~50cm/px, GSD 0.65m/px). RapidEye is deprecated/ retired.

Solutions:

Planet Monitoring | Planet Basemaps | Planet Tasking | Plant Analytic Feeds

My learnings.

The legacy player in the market. Been in this space for a decade. Offers multiple products/services. Planet Explorer is their platform.

Planet Analytics - Service that uses AI / ML to automatically detect, analyze and alert based on satellite imagery. Monitors the earth’s surface over time to automatically alert on changes that include vegetation changes, landscape changes, etc.

More helpful for general/ common scenarios where the platform will automatically analyze large image data sets.

Planet Monitoring Services - Provide data sets to specific industries & specific needs. Clients can request satellite images in a specific time frame over a specific geographic location, and make their own analysis. The tool will help them with tailored/curated information, and what they derive out of that is customer/ industry specific.

2. Orbital SideKick

  1. Founded in 2016, based in the USA.
  1. ~Revenue <5M
  1. Technology- hyperspectral imaging.
  1. Has its own satellites in orbit ~ 6 in the Global Hyperspectral Observation Satellites (GHOSt) constellation. Each GHOSt satellite has a proprietary hyperspectral imager manufactured by OSK. The sensor is developed to capture 512 spectral bands across the visible (VIS) to shortwave infrared (SWIR) region.
  1. Their satellites are higher in mass compared to Pixxel’s. And has an orbit life expectancy of 5 years.

They say - ‘OSK is the only HSI provider with proprietary satellites and detection tools designed to help optimize sustainable operations in real-time’. How? Yet to get this clarified.

Spectral Intelligence Global Monitoring Application

  1. SIGMA Monitor - Analyse historical data over a period of time and use AI /ML to analyze and provide valuable insights, and also automatically alert.
  1. SIGMA Data - Only gives the satellite imagery data sets - users can request and make their own investigation

Fun fact - GHOSt's predecessor was called ‘Aurora’

4. Maxar Technologies

  1. Founded in 1968 - Public Company
  1. Another legacy player with 50+ years in this space.
  1. Has had a series of satellites in orbit since 1989. Not sure of the numbers. Giving them the advantage of more historical data.
  1. On-demand intelligence - has separate products for different use cases.
  1. Since they are old, they didn’t build their own AI/ML-powered platform. To catch up, acquired players in the industry.
